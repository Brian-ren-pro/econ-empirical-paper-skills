# Section Benchmark Rubric

Use this checklist when comparing a manuscript section against top-journal exemplars.

## Abstract

- Does the first sentence state the economic question, not a broad truism?
- Does the institutional shock appear only after its economic function is clear?
- Are there 2-3 core results rather than a table-by-table result list?
- Is the final sentence a positive contribution claim rather than a defensive caveat?
- Are data sources omitted unless essential to credibility?

## Introduction

- Does paragraph 1 establish the puzzle and why it matters?
- Does paragraph 2 state the research question plainly?
- Does the setting enter as a solution to identification or measurement, not as background trivia?
- Are results previewed early enough?
- Are contributions organized by intellectual margin rather than literature bucket labels?

## Literature Positioning

- Does the section read as an argument rather than a bibliography?
- Are cited literatures connected through the paper's mechanism?
- Does each paragraph end by showing the gap this paper fills?
- Are hypothesis-style claims used only if standard for the target field?

## Data And Sample

- Does the section identify each database's economic object?
- Is the unit of observation explicit?
- Are matching and timing rules described enough to judge look-ahead bias?
- Are sample restrictions stated as research design choices, not cleaning steps?
- Are sample sizes tied to table roles and observation units?
- Are auxiliary datasets framed by what they identify or measure?

## Empirical Design

- Is the treatment timing and variation source explicit?
- Is the fixed-effect structure tied to threats to identification?
- Is clustering justified by treatment variation?
- Are limitations stated without weakening the main design unnecessarily?

## Results

- Does each table paragraph start with what the table proves?
- Are coefficients interpreted economically where possible?
- Are mechanism tables distinguished from main causal evidence?
- Are robustness checks grouped by threat addressed?
- Are null or weaker results written honestly without overexplaining?

## Conclusion

- Does it synthesize the paper's answer rather than repeat all results?
- Does it state the contribution in market/mechanism terms?
- Are welfare or policy implications bounded by the evidence?
