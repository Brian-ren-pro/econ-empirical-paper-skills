# Reviewer Panel Roles

## Editor-in-Chief

Perspective: top-field empirical finance editor.

Focus:

- research question;
- contribution and novelty;
- fit with finance/banking/law-and-finance readership;
- whether identification supports the claims;
- whether mechanism language is disciplined;
- publishability and revision strategy.

## Identification Reviewer

Focus:

- treatment/shock definition;
- identifying variation;
- parallel trends and pre-trends;
- staggered DID issues;
- policy confounds;
- sample selection;
- interpretation limits.

This reviewer is strict about causal language.

## Empirical Methods Reviewer

Focus:

- data construction;
- variable definitions;
- fixed effects;
- clustering;
- table design;
- robustness and sensitivity checks;
- result reproducibility from supplied materials.

## Field Reviewer

Choose one profile:

- Corporate Finance Reviewer.
- Banking / Financial Intermediation Reviewer.
- Law & Finance / Institutions Reviewer.
- Applied Corporate Empirics Reviewer.

Focus:

- literature placement;
- field language;
- contribution relative to field standards;
- missing obvious core references;
- whether evidence speaks to the claimed subfield.

## Devil's Advocate

Focus:

- strongest alternative explanation;
- mechanism overreach;
- contribution overclaiming;
- simpler risk, litigation, state-policy, macro, or sample-composition stories;
- whether the central claim survives skeptical reading.

The Devil's Advocate should not duplicate small reviewer comments. It should attack the strongest version of the paper.
