# Editorial Decision Standards

## accept-like

Use rarely. Requires:

- strong contribution;
- credible identification;
- no critical mechanism overclaim;
- complete evidence package;
- only minor writing or framing issues.

## minor revision

Use when:

- core design and contribution are credible;
- revisions are mostly framing, clarity, table presentation, or moderate additions;
- no critical Devil's Advocate issue remains.

## major revision

Use when:

- paper has publishable potential;
- core contribution is plausible;
- identification, framing, mechanism discipline, or evidence hierarchy needs substantial work;
- revisions may change the manuscript materially.

This is common for serious internal pre-submission review.

## reject-like

Use when:

- contribution is unclear or already known;
- identification cannot support the central claims;
- main evidence is missing or contradicted;
- mechanism story depends on unsupported assumptions;
- no realistic revision path exists with current materials.

## EIC Synthesis Rules

- Do not fabricate reviewer comments.
- Distinguish consensus from disagreement.
- If Devil's Advocate finds a critical issue, no `accept-like`.
- Provide a prioritized revision roadmap.
- Include "do not over-revise" cautions when reviewers ask for scope-expanding additions.
